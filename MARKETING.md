# Marketing Ideas


For my products that crossed the 100-user mark, the mix was always the same:

\- Launched on Product Hunt, BetaList, TAAFT, SaasHub, etc + 2-3 category-specific directories in week 1. Combined those brought in over 200 cold visitors over 3 weeks and single digit amount of paying customers in first weeks.

\- Cold-emailed 200-300 specific people who fit the buyer profile. Manual research, not blasts. About 10% replied, 2-3% converted to a paid call.

\- Sat in 2-3 communities where my buyer already complained about the problem. Daily comments for 60 days, no promotion. DMs and signups started arriving in month 2. Exhausting, but effective if done right.

\- Wrote 5-10 SEO articles in the first 60 days targeting specific buyer questions. None ranked in the first 90 days. Several started bringing dozens of visits per month by month 4-5.

\- Talked to at least the first 30 paying users by name - in DMs and on zoom. Recorded calls, asked what almost made them not buy. Used those quotes in the next batch of cold emails and SEO articles.

No single channel did it. By user 100 there were always 4-5 channels contributing 10-30 users each, plus referrals from the first paying users.

The first 30 paying-user conversations always make me rewrite our landing page
Also, stuff that worked for one project, somehow does not work for another. That's the reason for multiple channels at the same time


B2B specifically, the channels that have actually delivered for me:

\- Cold email to a tight list of 100-200 specific prospects per cycle. First line referencing something specific about their business. Reply rate 5-10% if the personalization is real.

\- LinkedIn + X commenting under your buyer's posts. Daily, 15-30 minutes, useful comments only. Slow at first. DMs warm up dramatically after 30 days of consistent presence.

\- SEO articles answering buyer-intent questions. 2-3 months to start ranking, 6 months to compound. Heavy filter now because AI-content detectors flag and demote auto-generated stuff. Has to be real writing.

\- Catalogues. Product Hunt, BetaList, TAAFT, SaaSHub, etc, niche category directories. Each one is a few hours of work and brings 20-50 cold-traffic visitors per listing in week 1.

\- Online events with a working chat. Show up, drop one useful comment per session, DM 3-5 people the same day to continue the conversation.

\- Offline events with an event app. Outreach directly through the app while still on-site, not after.

\- Partnerships with someone who already has your buyer audience and a complementary offering. Higher conversion than any cold channel because the trust transfers.

On each product launch we always have a referral program - but results are mixed. Some work good. Some never picked up.
Same with the mist of channels. Things that worked for one product, somehow do not work for another. So that's why we always try to launch several things in parallel, to see which ones work this specific time
